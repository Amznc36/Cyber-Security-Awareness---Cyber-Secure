Cases

EarthLink Spammer 2000

One of the first botnet attacks to be acknowledged by the public, it was created to send a large amount of Phishing emails masked as emails from legitimate websites and companies this spammer was able to send over 1.25 million malicious emails to gather sensitive information

WannaCry Ransomware 2017

This Variant of ransomware was known as one of the most damaging attacks in recent history, it’s estimated that this attack was valued a 4 billion dollars in losses with an average cost of $300 per release of machine. the WannaCry ransomware virus spread worldwide through computer operating the Microsoft windows operating system, Files were encrypted and held hostage by the attackers that demanded a payment in Bitcoin. According to an article “were it not for the continued use of outdated computer systems and poor education around the need to update software, the damage caused by this attack could have been avoided” The attackers responsible for this ransomware exposed a weakness in the windows operating system using a hack known as EternalBlue which was made public by a group of hackers known as the Shadow Brokers, according to the Microsoft in response to this hack Microsoft released a security patch which protected users systems against the EternalBlue hack however many individuals and enterprises in the world don’t regularly patch and update the operating system’s and as a result those who don’t were left exposed to the hack. The variant of ransomware affected various individuals and enterprises, companies such as the NHS took a heavy hit from the spread of the software, according to new sources the NHS at the Time of the Ransomware attack were still utilizing the windows xp operating system across thousands of their computers in the uk which Microsoft stopped supporting back on April 8 2014 and only released a patch as an exception because of the widespread of the wannacry ransomware

PhoneSpy 2021

In 2021 some Researcher from Zimperium zLabs publishes a report detailing a spyware app they had identified in South Korea that was targeting android devices connected to the internet, this spy disguised itself as an alternate application to gain access to infected machine, remotely control it, and compromise data. According to an investigation by the lab “PhoneSpy was found in 23 legitimate-seeming apps like Yoga learning, video streaming and messaging apps” [4] These apps they discovered the spyware in were not ones belonging to the google play store which led the research to believe that the cause of its spread was through other third party platforms

Dyn Attack 2016

Back in 2016 the enterprise Dyn was hit by a massive DDoS attack that has been regarded as one of the biggest DDoS attacks ever due to it broad impact, Dyn is a major DNS provider, and many major enterprises use DYN’s services to connect domain names back to the correct IP addresses, many of the sites using its services were also disrupted by the attack including Airbnb, Netflix, Paypal, Amazon. This DDos attack was carried out using malware known as Mirai, the Mirai program can create botnets out of infected IOT Devices such as cameras and smart tvs to create attack traffic. According to an article back in 2016 “The DDoS attack on Dyn was actually a series of attacks that took place at different times throughout the day Friday and affected different set of customers” [5] another factor that was mentioned by this article is that ““the attack involved 10s of millions of IP addresses” making it highly distributed and sophisticated. Dyn concluded from their investigation of the attack that IoT is to blame for this attack as Mirai program took advantage of IoT devices with default usernames and passwords to turn them into bots used to carry cyber-attacks.
